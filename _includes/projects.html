Current Projects

Martian Moons

I am developing an N-body dynamics accretion model called Swiftest, that uses fragmentation, tidal evolution, and ring/moons evolution to reconcile observable properties of Phobos and Deimos with their formation via giant-Martian impact debris disk hypothesis.

The Importance of Being Swiftest: The effects of collisional fragmentation on the accretion timescale of the martian moons and the terrestrial planets. (Talk) Pouplin, J., Wishard, C., Elliott, J., Minton, D. (2020)
Swiftest: An N-body dynamics code incorporating collisional regime determination and fragmentation (Poster) Wishard, C., Pouplin, J., Elliott, J., Minton, D. (2020
Evidence for a Past Martian Ring from the Orbital Inclination of Deimos (Paper) Cuk, M., Minton, D., Pouplin, J., Wishard., C.
The origin of Miranda from a massive early ring at Uranus. (Talk) Minton D., Hesselbrock, A., Pouplin, J.

Chariklo and other TNOs
